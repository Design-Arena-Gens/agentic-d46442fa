Weapon Structural Anatomy

The Model 1892 inherits strong locking from earlier Winchester designs, scaled for pistol cartridges. Core assemblies and recommended mesh splits for a riggable, game?ready asset:

  • Barrel Assembly: octagonal or round barrel variants; front sight dovetail; crown geometry.
  • Receiver: houses bolt, dual vertical locking lugs, and carrier (lifter).
  • Bolt: reciprocates via lever; interfaces with extractor and locking lugs.
  • Lever: primary user interaction; drives bolt and carrier linkages.
  • Carrier (Lifter): raises cartridges from magazine to chamber level.
  • Loading Gate: spring?loaded side gate feeding the tube magazine.
  • Magazine Tube: under?barrel; cap, follower, and retaining band(s).
  • Buttstock & Forearm: oiled walnut; buttplate (often steel) and barrel band(s).
  • Rear Sight: buckhorn/ladder types; elevation steps, leaf spring.
  • Small Hardware: screws, pins, springs; highly visible wear points.

Mechanical Component Details

Cycle of Operation

  1. Lever opens ? bolt retracts; dual lugs unlock; extractor pulls case.
  2. Carrier drops to accept a new cartridge from magazine.
  3. Lever closes ? carrier lifts cartridge to chamber axis.
  4. Bolt drives forward, chambers round, lugs lock; hammer engagement.

Animation/Constraints

  • Lever rotates around front receiver pin; link drives bolt translation.
  • Carrier follows a guided arc/translation; loading gate flexes on spring.
  • Rear sight leaf pivots; ladder detents optional for close?up rigs.

Topology Guardrails

  • Support loops at contact edges (lever/receiver, bolt/raceways).
  • Avoid long, thin triangles where normals will bake from tight radii.
  • Chamfer widths match real tool radii (0.3?1.0 mm typical).
  • Keep screw heads quadded for clean bake and anisotropic wear.

Material Texture Characteristics

Metals

  • Blued Steel: mid?high metalness, low roughness (0.1?0.25), subtle cool tint; edge burnishing to ~0.05.
  • Case?Hardened Steel: mottled carburation pattern; slightly higher roughness variance; thin?film color hints (very subtle).
  • Bare Wear: brightened edges on lever, loading gate lip, screw slots; directional micro?scratches.

Wood

  • Oiled Walnut: anisotropic grain; roughness 0.35?0.55 with hand?polished low?R streaks.
  • Compression dents at sling/band contact; finish thin at high?touch zones.

Wear Masking Map Stack

  • Curvature?based edge highlight (soft threshold).
  • AO?modulated grime in undercuts (receiver flats, screw heads).
  • Directional handling polish on lever underside and wrist.
  • Dust in sight steps and wood pores (very subtle normal noise).

Manufacturing Technique Representations

  • Forging & Milling: reflect historical toolpaths with consistent chamfers and fillets; avoid CNC?sharp features.
  • Color Case Hardening: replicate irregular blotches along flats/edges; keep saturation restrained.
  • Bluing: uniform but shows thinness at high edges; include heat?tint near muzzle from repeated firing (optional).
  • Hand?Fitting Marks: faint draw?file or stoning patterns on internal, seldom?seen surfaces.
  • Wood Finishing: pore?filled but not glassy; end?grain slightly darker and rougher.

Game?Ready 3D Modeling Pipeline

1) References & Scale

  • Collect orthographic museum photos; align to consistent DPI; set real?world scale.
  • Establish master measurements (barrel length, receiver width, sight heights).

2) High?Poly

  • Model manufacturable bevels (tool radius real?world plausible).
  • Boolean then remesh/polish for clean bake?ready surfaces.

3) Low?Poly

  • Target tris: FP 35?55k; TP 12?25k (variant?dependent).
  • Split moving parts; maintain silhouette on barrel/receiver fillets.

4) UVs

  • Texel density: FP 1024 px / 10 cm; TP 512 px / 10 cm.
  • Seams along hard edges and shadow lines; pack by part priority.

5) Baking

  • MikkTSpace; cage with 1?2 mm offset; anti?alias ?2?4.
  • Name matching for part?specific bakes (e.g., _high/_low suffix).

6) Texturing

  • PBR metal/rough; blued steel base, layered oxide tints, restrained edge polish.
  • Wood: scanned walnut base, pore detail normal, oil sheen gradient from handling.

7) Rig & Animation

  • Set accurate pivots (lever pin, sight leaf, loading gate flex).
  • Author firing/reload cycles; integrate with IK hand targets.

8) LODs & Integration

  • LOD1 ~50?60% tris; LOD2 ~25?35% tris; collapse micro fasteners.
  • Collision: compound boxes for receiver/barrel; narrow capsule for lever.
